Prototype Writeup

What have you done so far?

We finished our proposal and have been building out the prototype for "Visual History of Wildfire Destruction in California." Our main question is how wildfire destruction in California has changed from 2015 to 2025. We pulled our data from CAL FIRE's incident archive (fire.ca.gov/incidents), which logs every wildfire over 10 acres with details like name, county, coordinates, acres burned, containment percentage, and structures destroyed. So far the prototype has two D3 visualizations: a map of California where each fire is a circle sized by acres burned, and a stacked bar chart showing acres burned per year broken down by five California regions. The two views are linked, so you can click a year button or a bar and both charts update together. That makes it easy to compare where fires hit hardest in a given year and which regions took the most damage.

What will be the most challenging part of your project to design, and why?

Honestly, the hardest part is going to be showing how big these fires are without the visualization turning into a mess. Some fires burn hundreds of thousands of acres and others burn like 15, so we keep going back and forth on whether to use a square-root scale, a log scale, or something else for the circle sizes, since each one has tradeoffs. We also want to add a county-level breakdown eventually, and getting the map, the bar chart, and a county view to all talk to each other smoothly is going to take some work. On top of that, we have over 3,000 fire records, and once you add tooltips, transitions, and filtering on all of them, keeping things fast on a normal laptop is not a given. That's probably where we'll spend most of our time in the next few weeks.
